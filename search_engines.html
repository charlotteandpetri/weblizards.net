Advertising your web site can seem quite daunting to the inexperienced. Not because it is necessarily difficult, but because a mistake can have significant and detrimental consequences. It is also very time consuming, especially when talking about registering the web site to the search engines on the web.

First, try to identify your customers. Who are they, where are they and what are they interested in. This of course is a huge topic that entire industries are devoted to answering. Find out who they are, then target this group as closely as possible. Every ad that is seen by someone outside of this target audience is a waste of your money.

Secondly, try to get someone that enjoys sales and marketing involved with your operation. Companies are usually specialists in a particular field. They are great designers, software developers, wine merchants etc., but not necessarily good promoters. If you don't enjoy it not only will you likely be bad at it, but you will also waste a lot of the time you could otherwise devote to the things that you are good at.

Whether finding sales and marketing help is possible or not, it is still important to have a good grasp of what is going on. Advertising can often be the single biggest expense you have. You need to know where the money is going.

If you have a limited advertising budget, spending it all on an ad campaign that fails to produce results can easily and often does put many companies out of business.

Search engine registration is crucial because it is the best way to attract new visitors even if you have a lot money to spend on a national or global advertising campaign. It is the best way because generally people who are on the Internet are searching for something. Internet hasn’t quite taken TV’s place as a leisure entertainment media yet. So, when they are searching for something that your company does or sells your web site should pop-up in the search.

Search engines can also be considered as an aid to find something you saw on TV, but can't quite remember what was the name of the company or the new product they are selling. (Taken that this company you are looking for, exist on the internet)

Factors to Consider

Well. It is easy to say it should pop-up in the search, but it is not that easy to implement. There are many factors that make some sites rank higher than others:

  • One thing that we can affect is how the site is designed and built. We know all the tricks that search engines like in the website’s code in order to list higher.
  • The other thing that can be affected is how to choose such item that will be embedded into your web site’s code such as: search keywords , your web site’s description, web site’s title which can be different from the site’s domain name, and last but not least the domain name for you site.
  • Keywords - You select the search terms that are relevant to your site, words and phrases that you think people would type when searching your site
  • Description - A short (15-25 words) description of why people should go to your site
  • Title - Name of the site and maybe one keyword if the name is not a keyword as well
  • Domain name - Catchy, meaningful (like a keyword) easy to remember name/address for the site

Some Other Promotion Options

Internet Banner Ads:
Should be the obvious choice for most Internet companies. Their glamour was severely reduced when they failed to live up to their expectations. They are now so pervasive on Web Sites that they are easily ignored. The trouble appears to be that there are far too many web sites hoping to sell them and not enough that want to use them. This however can present a significant opportunity. When the supply is high and the demand is low, prices come down. Advertised prices seem to be all over the place but my guess is that there is a lot of room for negotiation. Expect to pay about $1000 to start but negotiate the number of clicks or impressions.

To-Do Email Newsletters:
Newsletters can either be very targeted or general. Non-targeted newsletter ads are selling for $4-$8 per 1000 subscribers. Targeted newsletters are selling from $5-$15 dollars per 1000. Most newsletters charge a flat fee but some will accept clicks similar to banner ads. Prices start at under $100.

Not-To-Do Email Newsletters:
Better known as Spam just don't seem to want to go away. They consequently appear to be effective for someone. Because of their nature however, it does not need many Spam Advertisers to flood our email boxes. I suspect that they are most popular with failing businesses desperate to try anything.


Here are some Traditional Options:

Trade Shows:
Usually a very targeted but relatively small audience. Not everyone at Comdex is likely to walk past your 10x10 booth. Trade shows can be very expensive costing anywhere from a minimum of a few thousand dollars to well into the hundred's of thousands of dollars. Because of the relatively small but highly target audience, a trade show is usually a better place to make strategic alliances than sales. Some Flee Market type shows are the exception. In any case, they will demand a significant amount of your time.

Trade Magazines:
Also usually caters to a highly targeted national audience. Prices again vary from a few hundred dollars to $40-$100,000+ depending on the circulation, size of the ad, ad location etc. Expect to spend a few thousand dollars to get something worthwhile. Using magazine advertising is easily overlooked but can be very affective for the right products.

Newspaper Ads:
A non-targeted local audience. Prices again vary depending on the size and location of the ad but you can again expect to spend a few thousand dollars for something with a presence. Because of the local, non-targeted audience, newspaper ads are better used by local "bricks and mortar" operations than by cyber space only businesses.

Radio:
Radio is similar in its coverage to local newspapers. Prices again vary depending on the audience size and the time of day. Radio has become popular with many ".COM" advertisers recently but I suspect it will pass. You had better have a good URL that is very easy to remember.

TV:
TV is very expensive, not only for the time spot but for the cost of the video. For a few hundred dollars I suppose you could always use your home Camcorder to shoot your mother and daughter for a 3 AM spot on a small station. TV is better left to multinational corporations looking for product or corporate branding.

Ad Agency:
Will create and coordinate your advertising campaign, buy advertising slots and do all kinds of other wonderful things for you. Until recently you could get their interest for a little under a $100,000. With all the money being spent by Internet IPO financed companies it is a lot harder to hire their services these days. Once the IPO money is gone it will probably return to normal; which is still far too expensive for most of us.

No Guarantees
Every now and then I hear somebody promising that they can list anybody's site in the search engines so that it will come up on the first page on the search. Well, that is a sales lie. Nobody can guarantee anything with these search engines, unfortunately. They can change all their criterias or drop every site that has blue background if they wish. Well, fortunately the latter is not what they are in business for. They are in business to list everybody's site in a easily searchable search engine. They want to keep their search engine as functional and objective to searches as possible, which is why they hate registerings done by software for example.
